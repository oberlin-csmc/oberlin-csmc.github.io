From: The Computer Science Majors' Committee

To: Robert Geitz, Rhys Price Jones, Chris Koch, Fritz Reuhr, and Rich Salter

Regarding: Departmental Hiring Policies

Date: October 13, 1994

We are concerned about the way that students are hired for positions within the computer science department. All too often, it seems that the decision to hire a student depends upon the student's "networking" skills rather than the student's ability to do the work well. We would like to suggest a few guidelines and ideas for student hiring that we hope will be followed in the future.

  • All positions should be publicly advertised, at least by a post to msgs. However, because not everyone reads msgs, it would be very useful to also have a bulletin board in the King lab and a newsgroup (oberlin.jobs.offered? or we could create oberlin.cs.jobs.offered) where open student positions are posted regularly.

  • Whenever possible, positions for the next semester should be advertised at least before the end of the current semester. Otherwise, it is difficult for students to plan their job schedules. This is especially a problem for students who need to make a certain amount of money each semester due to financial issues. If they can't count on a cs job, they have to go and get a job at cds (or somewhere else), only to find out a few weeks later that there is a job offered in cs that they would have been perfect for. This also means that a professor is not looking for a grader at the last minute, and assignments can get graded on time even at the beginning of the semester.

  • There should be a reasonable time between when a job offer is posted and when the decision to hire is made - a week at minimum would be a good guideline. Otherwise, students are not given enough time to decide whether the job fits in with their schedule, etc. It would be a benefit both to the students and the department if hiring were merit based, rather than first come first serve.
