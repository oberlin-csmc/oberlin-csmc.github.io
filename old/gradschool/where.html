What to do if you have no clue what you want to study or where

For those of you with no idea what you want to do, I suggest looking around at random schools. Read about their research projects, and see if any of them interest you. If a project interests you, download a paper about it and read that. Then go look for other projects that might be similar or in the same area at other schools. The schools which have lots of interesting projects are the ones you want to apply to :).

You might also try going to the ACM Computing Week conference. In person. At that conference, you'll have a chance to hear people talk about their research in many different areas. In person. And see how excited they are about what they're doing.

If you're really unsure about what you want to do, remember that grad school is not your only option. In fact, if you're not absolutely sure that you want to spend another 2 (masters) or 6 (phd) years in school, you want to think very carefully about whether grad school is right for you.

How to rate schools

It's generally a bad idea to just look at a survey-based ranking of schools in order to determine whether or not a certain school will fit your needs. On the other hand, a ranking might be able to help you get a feel for whether or not you're applying to some schools which are easy to get into and some which aren't. The best thing to do is to ask some faculty for help judging this, but there's as a web site with a Ranking of PhD Programs in Computer Science.
