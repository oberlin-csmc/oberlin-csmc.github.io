Tips (courtesy of Michael A. Covington [mcovingt@ai.uga.edu])

  • If you want personal attention from the graduate coordinator, please give personal attention _to_ the graduate coordinator. Don't send a single form letter simultaneously to 200 institutions half of which you know nothing about. Look in Peterson's Guides and similar resources to try to identify appropriate universities, and find out something about them before you write to them.
  • Realize that American institutions, in general, will not accept or reject you until you pay the $25 application fee. (However, we _can_ tell you in advance whether you are missing any clear minimum requirements such as appropriate undergraduate degrees and the like.)
  • Even at minor universities, admission in computer-related fields is surprisingly competitive, and assistantships are even more competitive.

Here's an example of a standard letter you could send to each email address:

To: gradinfo@cs.columbia.edu
Subject: Gradschool Application & Information
FCC: .FCC
--text follows this line--

Hi,

My name is Jen Mankoff, I'm a student at Oberlin College and I'm looking for some information about Columbia's graduate computer science program. Specifically, could you tell me where I could find out more information about the research areas of your professors? Also, could you please send me an application for admission to your Ph.D. program and any other pertinent information?

My adress is:

Jen Mankoff
64 N. Park
Oberlin, OH 44074
