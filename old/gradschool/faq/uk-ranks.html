One thing to remember in this list is that the Universities have been ranked according to the following factors (the separate points division is shown in the newspaper) :

  1. Entry requiremnts and Grades (Cambridge = 99/100 *)
  2. Student/Staff Ratio (Cambridge = 97/100)
  3. Research, Development & Consultancy Income (Imperial College = 85/90)
  4. Staff with PhDs (Cambridge & Imperial College = 35/35)
  5. Percentage of Staff with professional Qualifications (Middlesex & Central Lancashire = 25/25)
  6. Library Spending (Oxford = 99/100)
  7. Student Accomodation (Keele = 55/55)
  8. Completion of Degrees Rates (Cambridge & Reading = 99/100)
  9. Number of Firsts (degree results) ** (Imperial College = 65/65)
  10. Research (Cambridge = 89/90)
  11. Value added to the student's life (Bradford & Westminster = 99/100)
  12. Postgraduates (LSE & City = 20/20)
  13. Employment (Brighton = 85/100)
  14. International Students (LSE = 20/20)
* The University in brackets is the one with the highest points for that specific factor

** The Degree system in the UK gives the Grades as follows (different from the system in the US or Canada where the GPA and MPA are used) :

  • First ===> Highest Awarded Grade
  • Upper Second
  • Lower Second
  • Third
  • Pass ===> Lowest Awarded Grade
