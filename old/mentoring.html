Proposal for CS Department Mentoring System

Purpose

To facilitate communication and information sharing between early (CS 151) students and people who know their way around the system; provide another mechanism for new students to learn.

Becoming involved

  • People can voluntarily sign up to be mentors or mentees; the system will be on a volunteer basis only.

  • Mentors will be assigned mentees by random assignment.

    If there are more mentees than mentors, a given mentor may be assigned more than one mentee, but only if the mentor has indicated that this would be acceptable.

  • If there are more mentors than mentees, some mentors will not be assigned specific mentees. This does not preclude them from participating in the program.

Informing people of the system, and expectations

  • A one-page handout, "How to be a mentor", should be produced. Basically, it should give the basics of how not to blow people away with too much information at one time.

  • Another one-page handout, "What to expect from your mentor", should also be produced. Basically, it should say that your mentor won't do your homework for you, will answer any CS-related question otherwise, etc.

  • A representative of the CSMC or Mentoring group should go in to 151 classes and make an announcement that the system is available, giving a brief description.

  • msgs should also be employed as a way to encourage people to sign up to be mentors or mentees.

Introducing Mentors to Mentees

  • A meeting/party should be held once assignments have been made, and all Mentors and Mentees should be encouraged to attend. The idea here is not only to introduce specific mentor/mentee pairs to each other, but also to promote mixing between the groups and to let mentees know which other mentoring people are available for questions.

Mentoring Coordinator

  • Someone will be responsible for coordinating this, mostly at the beginning making assignments, etc.
