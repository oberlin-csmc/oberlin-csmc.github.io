This is our space
and we should make an effort
to keep it clean, safe, and functional.

  • Please be careful with food and drink.
    If possible, use closed/closable containers.

    If you do spill, clean it up immediately
    and if it damages equipment,
    please do what you can to help replace it.

    The lounge outside the lab is a better place to eat.

  • Before you leave the lab,
    make sure you have thrown away any trash
    you may have created
    and put away any books you've taken out.

  • If you are the last person to leave the lab,
    please make sure the lights are off,
    the windows are closed and latched,
    and the door is closed.

  • Computer Science students are the only students
    with 24-hour access to King.

    Please don't let anyone without a lab pass into King after hours.

  • If you bring visitors into the lab
    you are responsible for them
    while they are here.

    Please inform them of the lab policies.

  • If you want to allow a guest to use a computer
    you must log them in using your account.

    If there aren't enough computers for everyone,
    please give CS students priority over visitors.

  • You are fully responsible for anything
    that happens on your account.

    Please do not leave yourself logged in and unlocked.
    If you leave the room, either lock your machine or log out.

  • Please do not leave yourself locked on a machine
    for an inordinate amount of time.

    The lab machines are for everyone's use.
    If you are running a long process,
    leave a note next to the machine.

  • If you think that someone has been locked
    for an unreasonable amount of time,
    contact them.

    Do not kill their session or restart the computer.

    In the unlikely case that you have no option
    but to kill someone's session,
    check first to see if they are running any processes
    (ps aux | grep username)
    so you do not interrupt something important.
